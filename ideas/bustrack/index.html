Bustrack

Description

(this chapter needs to be rewritten: 1.describe the problem, 2.Buses are available, 3.Promote by use by offering tracking/booking) Although there is not much traffic on Saba, many people own cars, with some families having multiple vehicles. Reducing the number of cars could be a reasonable goal for both environmental and cost-saving reasons.

Globally, combustion vehicles are gradually being replaced by more environmentally friendly alternatives. While electric vehicles are becoming more prevalent worldwide, they are not as common on Saba. Since Saba generates electricity locally, charging electric cars could become problematic if people continue to drive them individually. To reduce the individual use of vehicles, the island already operates a free public bus. We propose to promote the use of the bus by offering a system that allows users to better plan their trips. Buses can drive according to a fixed schedule, but if exceptions occur, passengers can see where the nearest bus is.

  1. Buses will get a GPS tracker
  2. GPS data will be transmitted to a central server and published on a website
  3. Users can install an app on their phone and see where the bus is
  4. (?) Users can pinpoint positions on the map and ask for pickup at a certain time
  5. (?) Instead of a Google Map, the map can be made in ‘metro-style’, a schematic representation of the route with checkpoints/busstops along the route
